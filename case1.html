Online invoicing

Giving small businesses more control of their cash flow and getting them paid faster.

Project overview

Invoicing is an important part of every business, itâ€™s what gets you paid. Businesses want to spend less time chasing payments.

During my time at MYOB the business had a goal of giving small businesses more control of their cash flow and getting them paid faster. This goal would focus on creating a online invoicing process that could be intergrated with several existing products.

Working within an agile scrum team we utilised Lean startup principles to iteratively build features and continually validate solutions with users.

Discover & Identify

Working with existing applications allowed the team to discover how the invoicing process currently works.

I led the following research -

  • Informal interviews with existing users to build empathy.
  • Interviews with support staff.
  • Interviews with stakeholders from applications which had invoicing functions.
  • Competitor analysis.

The team came together at inception to make our initial assumptions.

  • I illustrated the current user flows and pain points.
  • I used audio clips of user interviews to share empathy for users with the team.

Multiple hypotheses then began to form. These were prioritised according to effort required to validate and value added.

Fast validation

We created a register your interest for online invoicing. The assumed most needed features of the product got marketed to a segment of users. Users needed to connect their accounts via a signup page to register interest. Connecting actual accounts showed a real commitment and want for the features.

Create & Build

As we were working to lean startup principles, only the lightest weight deliverable was created to validate each hypotheses.

I was responsible for creating various testable and releasable deliverables including -

  • Wireframes
  • Prototypes - Invision, HTML & CSS
  • Finished UI designs
  • Deployable HTML & CSS

Designs incorporated -

  • Current best design practices
  • Qualitative and quantitative data from experiments
  • Company style guides
  • Collaboration with other designers and team members.

Deliverables were kept as live documentation as much as possible to stop the use of stale assets.

Tools - Sketching, Balsamiq, LucidChart, Photoshop, InVision, Sublime Text.

Building a BETA lab

A BETA product using APIs to send invoices from a major accounting app became the initial MVP. The build time was much faster by not disrupting the existing product. Experiments with real users and invoices gave great reliable quantitative data.

Experiment & Learn

Through experimentation we validated and refined ideas before going to far down any path.

I led the user testing sessions that were normally ran on a weekly basis -

  • Internal stakeholders testing onsite
  • Support staff testing on site
  • Real customers testing onsite and remotely
  • Real invoices being sent with BETA service

All sessions were open for all team members to attend utilising the on-site focus room if needed. The sessions were recorded and key results shared with documented quotes or video clips.

Tools - Camtasia, gotomeeting, InVision, Google Analytics, Intercom.

BETA experiments

Experiments in BETA have began to filter through to real products. Online invoice event tracking is now in IOS and Android apps.

Outcomes

Milestones through the iterative process-

  • Register interest to product with basic setup.
  • BETA service sending invoices for major accounting product.
  • Full Integration with mobile apps for sending invoices.

Currently the project is being fully implemented with other apps at MYOB.
